# Founder Agent Kit — Research Foundation

This agent is built on empirical research into startup failure patterns and established competency theory.

## Core Study

**Szathmári, E., Varga, Z., Molnár, A., Németh, G., Szabó, Z. P., & Kiss, O. E. (2024).** Why do startups fail? A core competency deficit model. *Frontiers in Psychology*, 15, 1299135. doi:10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1299135

**Key Findings (prevalence among the 50 analyzed failures):**

- 70% — Information-seeking deficit (did not consciously seek data for decisions)
- 66% — Customer service orientation deficit (prioritized tech solutions over customer problems)
- 38% — Technical / strategic-business-planning expertise deficit
- 36% — Analytical thinking deficit (could not confront data or draw rigorous conclusions)
- 36% — Flexibility deficit (did not pivot on evidence)

**Methodology:**

- 50 publicly shared post-mortem narratives by CEOs of failed startups
- Sourced in 2018; 47 of 50 failures occurred between 2014–2017
- 23 countries (20% California, 28% other US/Canada, 26% Europe, 22% Asia-Pacific, 2% Latin America)
- Modified Critical Incident Technique (Flanagan, 1954), 3 independent coders
- Coded against Spencer & Spencer's competency model (20 competencies, 6 clusters)
- Average operating duration of failed startups: 34 months

**The Five Competency Deficits (mapped to Fonda's five traits):**

1. Information-seeking → "Obsessive Information-Seeking"
2. Customer service orientation → "Customer-First Orientation"
3. Analytical thinking → "Structured Analytical Thinking"
4. Flexibility → "Evidence-Based Flexibility"
5. Technical / business-planning expertise → "Business Model Literacy"

Published open-access in *Frontiers in Psychology* (CC BY). DOI resolves to the full article.

## Design Rationale

**Why this personality?**
- Founders have drive, conviction, initiative (these do NOT appear as deficits in Szathmári et al., 2024)
- They lack structure, skepticism, rigor (these DO appear as dominant deficits)
- The agent complements deficits, doesn't mirror strengths
- Friction is the value — not obstruction, but protection

**Why mode switching?**
- Questioning strategy = value (targets the deficits that correlate with failure)
- Questioning execution = friction without value
- "I can do that" before questions = competence signal

**Why these four skills?**
- Customer Discovery — operationalizes Customer Service Orientation (trait #2, 66% deficit)
- Data Source Finder — operationalizes Information-Seeking (trait #1, 70% deficit)
- Market Assumption Tracker — operationalizes Analytical Thinking and Flexibility (traits #3 & #4, 36% each)
- Pivot Signal — operationalizes Flexibility (trait #4) and Business Model Literacy (trait #5, 38%)
